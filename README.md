## 📖 Overview

<a href="#">Genspark</a> addresses the specific inefficiency at the center of knowledge-intensive work: that forming an accurate, complete picture of any complex topic requires reading more sources than time allows, and the synthesis connecting those sources demands its own cognitive effort on top of the reading itself. Genspark handles both the reading and the synthesis, delivering the picture directly.

The <a href="#">source authority weighting</a> prioritizes primary sources, peer-reviewed content, and established expert publications in synthesis while contextualizing lower-credibility sources appropriately when they represent a significant viewpoint. Research output reflects source quality rather than treating all web content as equivalent. <a href="#">Persistent research library</a> saves sparkpages for ongoing access, allowing past research to be returned to and extended rather than repeated from scratch when a topic requires further investigation.

<a href="#">Shareable research documents</a> distribute sparkpages through links accessible without a Genspark account — a practical format for briefing colleagues and clients rather than forwarding lists of links or writing summaries. <a href="#">Formatted document export</a> downloads sparkpages as structured documents suitable for inclusion in reports, presentations, and documentation systems.

The <a href="#">topic change monitoring</a> alerts when significant new information appears on tracked subjects, keeping research current without periodic manual re-querying. <a href="#">Cross-language synthesis</a> reads and synthesizes sources across major languages, extending research coverage to non-English publications for topics where important source material exists internationally.

## 💬 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What is topic monitoring and how does it work?</summary>

Add a topic with a description of what developments matter. Genspark checks for new significant information on a configured schedule and delivers an alert summarizing what changed with links to new sources.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Genspark handle highly technical or specialized research topics?</summary>

Yes. Technical depth depends on source material availability. Peer-reviewed publications, technical documentation, and specialist commentary are included in synthesis when they exist for the topic.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can sparkpages be shared with people who do not have Genspark accounts?</summary>

Yes. Sparkpages generate shareable links viewable by recipients without logging in, making them practical for briefing colleagues and clients.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Genspark support research in languages other than English?</summary>

Multi-language support covers major languages. Research quality depends on the volume of available source material in the target language. Cross-language synthesis reads sources in multiple languages and produces output in the user's preferred language.

</details>
